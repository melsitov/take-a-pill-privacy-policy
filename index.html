1Introduction

Take a Pill ("the App", "we", "us") is a medicine reminder application built to help you track your medications and dosing schedules. This Privacy Policy explains what information the App accesses, how it is used, and how it is protected.

We are committed to protecting your privacy. Because health information is sensitive, the App is designed to keep your data on your device whenever possible.

2Data stored on your device

All data you enter is stored locally in an encrypted SQLite database on your device. Nothing is uploaded to our servers automatically.

  • Medicine information — name, dosage, unit, quantity, notes, barcode.
  • Schedules — reminder times, frequency, start and end dates.
  • Consumption history — whether a dose was taken, skipped, or snoozed, and when.
  • App settings — theme preference, language, quiet hours, snooze duration.

This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly share it (see Backup & Export below).

3External network requests

The App makes network requests only when you use the barcode scanner to look up a medicine. In that case, the scanned barcode number is sent to one or more of the following public databases to retrieve the medicine name and dosage:

Service What is sent Privacy policy
OpenFDA (api.fda.gov) Barcode number only open.fda.gov/privacy
UPC Item DB (upcitemdb.com) Barcode number only upcitemdb.com/privacy
Open Food Facts (openfoodfacts.org) Barcode number only openfoodfacts.org/privacy
Open Beauty Facts (openbeautyfacts.org) Barcode number only openbeautyfacts.org/privacy
Open Products Facts (openproductsfacts.org) Barcode number only openproductsfacts.org/privacy

No personal data, health history, or identifying information is ever transmitted to these or any other external service. The barcode lookup feature is entirely optional — you can add medicines manually without triggering any network request.

The App does not use any analytics SDK, crash reporting service, advertising network, or any other third-party data collection library.

4Camera and image processing

The App requests access to your device camera for two optional features:

  • Barcode scanning — reads the barcode on a medicine package. The camera image is processed locally; only the decoded barcode number is used.
  • Text (OCR) scanning — takes a photo of a medicine label and extracts the medicine name and dosage using on-device text recognition (Google ML Kit). The image is never uploaded anywhere; all OCR processing happens entirely on your device.

Photos taken for scanning are not saved to your gallery and are deleted after processing.

5Notifications

The App sends local push notifications to remind you to take your medicines at the scheduled times. These notifications are generated entirely on your device — no notification content (medicine names, times, etc.) is transmitted to any server.

You can disable notifications at any time through your device settings or within the App.

6Backup and export

The App provides an optional manual backup feature that exports your database to a single file on your device. You can share this file using your device's native share sheet (e.g. email, a cloud storage app, or a messaging app of your choice).

We have no access to your backup files. Where they are stored and who can access them is determined entirely by you and the service you choose to share them with.

Similarly, the PDF adherence report generated by the App is created locally and shared only if you choose to export it. We do not receive a copy.

7Device permissions

Permission Why it is needed

POST_NOTIFICATIONS Displaying medicine reminder notifications
SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM Delivering reminders at the precise time you set (Android 12+)
RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED Re-scheduling reminders automatically after the device restarts

USE_FULL_SCREEN_INTENT Showing a full-screen alarm notification for urgent reminders

The App does not request access to your location, contacts, microphone, call logs, calendar, or any other personal data source.

8Data retention and deletion

All data is stored locally on your device for as long as you use the App. You can delete individual medicines, schedules, and history entries from within the App at any time.

To delete all data, clear the App's storage in your device settings or uninstall the App. Because we do not store any data on our servers, there is nothing for us to delete on your behalf.

9Children's privacy

The App is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal data through the App, please contact us so we can address the situation.

10Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
